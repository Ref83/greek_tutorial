# Past Simple Tense (Αόριστος)

## The Concept
The **Aoristos** is the most common past tense in Greek. The word itself means "indefinite" or "unbounded," but in practice, it is used to describe **completed actions** that happened at a specific point in time in the past.

Think of it as a single "snapshot" or a "point" on a timeline, rather than a continuous or repeating video.

## Usage
We use the Aoristos when:
* The action happened **once**.
* The action is **finished**.
* We specify **when** it happened (e.g., yesterday, last week).

**Common time expressions:**
* **χθες** (yesterday)
* **πριν** (before/ago)
* **πέρσι** (last year)
* **την προηγούμενη εβδομάδα** (last week)

## The Mechanics
For regular verbs (First Conjugation, ending in **-ω**), the formation follows a specific "mechanical" change: **Augment + Stem Change + Ending.**

### 1. The Stem Change (The Sigma Rule)
Most regular verbs change their ending consonant before adding the past endings. The most common change is adding a **-σ-** (sigma).
* **-ω** becomes **-σα**
* *Example:* γράφ-ω (I write) → έ-γραψ-α (I wrote)

### 2. The Simple Past Endings
| Person | Ending | Example (γράφω) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| εγώ | **-α** | έγραψ**α** |
| εσύ | **-ες** | έγραψ**ες** |
| αυτός/ή/ό | **-ε** | έγραψ**ε** |
| εμείς | **-αμε** | γράψ**αμε** |
| εσείς | **-ατε** | γράψ**ατε** |
| αυτοί/ές/ά | **-αν** (or **-ανε**) | έγραψ**αν** |

### 3. The "Augment" (Το Πρόσφυμα)
If a verb has only two syllables in its past form (like γρά-ψα), we add an **ε-** at the beginning to keep the stress on the third syllable from the end. This is called the "augment."
* **Present:** γράφω (2 syllables)
* **Past:** **έ**γραψα (3 syllables - Stress moves to the "ε")
* **Note:** In "εμείς" and "εσείς," the augment drops because the word is already long enough: **γράψαμε**, not ~~εγράψαμε~~.

### 4. The Transformation Rules
Here is how different stems react when they meet the **-σ-** of the Past Simple:

| Stem Ending | Change + σ | Aoristos Ending | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **-π, -β, -φ** | **= ψ** | **-ψα** | λείπω → **έλειψα** |
| **-κ, -γ, -χ** | **= ξ** | **-ξα** | ανοίγω → **άνοιξα** |
| **-ζ, -θ, -τ** | **= σ** | **-σα** | διαβάζω → **διάβασα** |

> **Note:** The "augment" (**ε-**) is still added at the beginning of two-syllable verbs to keep the stress on the third syllable from the end.

---

## Exceptions
While many Greek verbs follow the "Sigma Rule" (changing the stem to **-σα**, **-ψα**, or **-ξα**), there is a group of **irregular verbs** that undergo a complete stem change in the **Aoristos**. These are often the most common verbs in daily conversation.

Here is a list of the most important irregular verbs and their transformation:

### Common Irregular Verbs
| Present Tense | Aoristos (Past Simple) | Meaning |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **βλέπω** | **είδα** | I saw |
| **λέω** | **είπα** | I said |
| **πίνω** | **ήπια** | I drank |
| **τρώω** | **έφαγα** | I ate |
| **έρχομαι** | **ήρθα** | I came |
| **φεύγω** | **έφυγα** | I left |
| **μπαίνω** | **μπήκα** | I entered |
| **βγαίνω** | **βγήκα** | I went out |
| **βρίσκω** | **βρήκα** | I found |
| **παίρνω** | **πήρα** | I took |

### Important Notes
* **Conjugation:** Even though the stems are irregular, these verbs usually take the standard **Aoristos endings** (**-α, -ες, -ε, -αμε, -ατε, -αν**) once the new stem is formed.
* **The "I" to "E" Change:** Many irregular verbs that start with a vowel in the past (like **είδα** or **ήρθα**) do not need an extra "augment" (**ε-**) because they already have enough syllables to place the stress correctly.
