# Present Tense: Group A Verbs (1st Conjugation)

## The Concept
In Greek, verbs are categorized into two main groups based on where the accent (stress) falls. **Group A** (1st Conjugation) consists of verbs where the accent falls on the **stem** (the root), and they end in an unaccented **-ω**.

## Usage
The Present Tense (*Ενεστώτας*) is used to describe:
* Actions happening **right now**.
* **Habitual actions** or routines.
* **General truths** or facts.

## The Mechanics
To conjugate these verbs, we remove the ending **-ω** to find the stem and add the personal endings that correspond to the subject.

### Conjugation Table: μένω (to stay/live)
The stem is **μεν-**.

| Person | Pronoun | Ending | Verb Form | Translation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 1st Sing. | εγώ | **-ω** | μέν**ω** | I stay / I am staying |
| 2nd Sing. | εσύ | **-εις** | μέν**εις** | you stay |
| 3rd Sing. | αυτός/ή/ό | **-ει** | μέν**ει** | he/she/it stays |
| 1st Plur. | εμείς | **-ουμε** | μέν**ουμε** | we stay |
| 2nd Plur. | εσείς | **-ετε** | μέν**ετε** | you (all) stay |
| 3rd Plur. | αυτοί/ές/ά | **-ουν(ε)** | μέν**ουν(ε)** | they stay |

### Sentence Forms
* **Positive:** Εγώ μένω στην Αθήνα. (I live in Athens.)
* **Negative:** **Δεν** μένω στην Αθήνα. (I do not live in Athens.)
* **Question:** Μένεις στην Αθήνα; (Do you live in Athens?)

### Examples of Common Group A Verbs
* **κάνω** (to do/make) $\rightarrow$ κάνω, κάνεις, κάνει...
* **γράφω** (to write) $\rightarrow$ γράφω, γράφεις, γράφει...
* **διαβάζω** (to read/study) $\rightarrow$ διαβάζω, διαβάζεις, διαβάζει...
* **παίζω** (to play) $\rightarrow$ παίζω, παίζεις, παίζει...
* **ακούω** (to listen/hear) $\rightarrow$ ακούω, ακούεις, ακούει...

## Exceptions & Notes
* **The "ν" in 3rd Plural:** The ending **-ουν** can often be seen as **-ουνε** in spoken Greek; both are correct.
* **Stem Stress:** In Group A, the stress remains on the same syllable of the stem throughout the conjugation (e.g., **κάν**-ω, **κάν**-ουμε).
* **The verb "είμαι" (to be):** Note that "to be" is irregular and does not follow this -ω pattern.
