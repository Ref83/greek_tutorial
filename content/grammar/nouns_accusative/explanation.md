# Accusative Case (Nouns)

## The Concept
The **Accusative Case** (Αιτιατική) is primarily used for the **direct object** of a sentence. While the Nominative case tells us *who* is doing the action (the subject), the Accusative tells us *what* or *who* is receiving that action.

## Usage
We use the Accusative case in three main scenarios:
1.  **Direct Objects:** To show the person or thing affected by a verb (e.g., "I see **the friend**").
2.  **After Prepositions:** Almost all common Greek prepositions (like **σε** - to/in, **για** - for, **με** - with) are followed by the Accusative.
3.  **Time Expressions:** To indicate duration or specific time (e.g., "every **Saturday**").

## The Mechanics
To form the Accusative, we must change the **Article** and often the **Ending** of the noun.

### 1. Masculine Nouns (ο → τον)
Masculine nouns lose the final **-ς** of the nominative.
* **Article change:** ο → **τον**
* **Ending change:** -ος/-ας/-ης → **-ο/-α/-η**

| Nominative | Accusative | English |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| ο φίλος | **τον φίλο** | the friend |
| ο πατέρας | **τον πατέρα** | the father |
| ο μαθητής | **τον μαθητή** | the student |

**Examples:**
* **Positive:** Βλέπω τον δάσκαλο. (I see the teacher.)
* **Negative:** Δεν βλέπω τον δάσκαλο. (I don't see the teacher.)
* **Question:** Βλέπεις τον δάσκαλο; (Do you see the teacher?)

---

### 2. Feminine Nouns (η → την)
Feminine nouns are easy—the noun ending usually stays the same as the nominative singular, but the article changes.
* **Article change:** η → **την**
* **Ending change:** Remains **-α** or **-η**

| Nominative | Accusative | English |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| η μητέρα | **την μητέρα** | the mother |
| η πόλη | **την πόλη** | the city |

**Examples:**
* Έχω μία φίλη. (I have a [female] friend.)
* Πηγαίνω στην (σε + την) τράπεζα. (I am going to the bank.)

---

### 3. Neuter Nouns (το → το)
Neuter nouns are the easiest of all! They **do not change** from Nominative to Accusative.
* **Article change:** Remains **το**
* **Ending change:** Remains **-ο/-ι/-μα**

| Nominative | Accusative | English |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| το σπίτι | **το σπίτι** | the house |
| το βιβλίο | **το βιβλίο** | the book |
| το όνομα | **το όνομα** | the name |

**Examples:**
* Διαβάζω το βιβλίο. (I am reading the book.)
* Θέλω ένα αυτοκίνητο. (I want a car.)

---

## Plural Accusative

In the plural, the pattern is similar: the **article changes** and masculine nouns also change their ending.

### 1. Masculine Plural (οι → τους)
The article changes from **οι** to **τους**. The noun ending depends on the original nominative ending.
* **-ος nouns:** plural nominative -οι → accusative **-ους**
* **-ας / -ης nouns:** plural ending **-ες** stays **-ες** (no change)

| Nominative Plural | Accusative Plural | English |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| οι φίλοι | **τους φίλους** | the friends |
| οι πατέρες | **τους πατέρες** | the fathers |
| οι μαθητές | **τους μαθητές** | the students |

**Examples:**
* Βλέπω τους φίλους μου. (I see my friends.)
* Ξέρω τους μαθητές. (I know the students.)

---

### 2. Feminine Plural (οι → τις)
The article changes from **οι** to **τις**. The noun ending stays exactly the same as in the nominative plural.
* **Article change:** οι → **τις**
* **Ending:** stays **-ες**

| Nominative Plural | Accusative Plural | English |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| οι μητέρες | **τις μητέρες** | the mothers |
| οι πόλεις | **τις πόλεις** | the cities |
| οι φίλες | **τις φίλες** | the friends (f.) |

**Examples:**
* Αγαπώ τις μητέρες. (I love the mothers.)
* Βλέπω τις φίλες μου. (I see my friends.)

---

### 3. Neuter Plural (τα → τα)
Neuter nouns in the plural **do not change at all** — the accusative plural is identical to the nominative plural.
* **Article:** stays **τα**
* **Ending:** unchanged

| Nominative Plural | Accusative Plural | English |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| τα σπίτια | **τα σπίτια** | the houses |
| τα βιβλία | **τα βιβλία** | the books |
| τα ονόματα | **τα ονόματα** | the names |

**Examples:**
* Διαβάζω τα βιβλία. (I am reading the books.)
* Θέλω τα σπίτια. (I want the houses.)

---

## Exceptions & Special Rules
* **The "N" Rule (τον/την):** In modern speech, the final **-ν** in **τον** and **την** is always kept if the next word starts with a vowel or certain consonants (κ, π, τ, ξ, ψ). If it starts with other consonants (like β, δ, γ, φ, θ, σ, ζ, λ, ρ, μ, ν), the **-ν** is often dropped in **την**, but usually kept in **τον** to distinguish it from the neuter **το**.
* **Nouns in -ος (Feminine):** Feminine nouns ending in **-ος** (like *η οδός*) follow the masculine pattern in the Accusative: **την οδό**.
