## Comparison of Adjectives (Σύγκριση Επιθέτων)

### The Concept
When we want to compare two people, objects, or ideas, we use the **Comparative** degree (e.g., "taller"). When we want to say something has the highest quality among a group, we use the **Superlative** degree (e.g., "the tallest").

### Usage
* **Comparative:** Used to compare two entities.
* **Superlative:** Used to distinguish one entity from a whole group.

### The Mechanics

There are two ways to form comparisons in Greek: **Periphrastic** (using extra words) and **Synthetic** (using suffixes).

#### 1. The Periphrastic Way (The easiest way!)
To make a comparative, simply add the word **πιο** (more) before the adjective. To show the "than" part, we use **από**.

* **Positive:** Ο Νίκος είναι ψηλός. (Nikos is tall.)
* **Comparative:** Ο Νίκος είναι **πιο ψηλός από** τον Γιώργο. (Nikos is taller than Giorgos.)
* **Superlative:** Ο Νίκος είναι **ο πιο ψηλός**. (Nikos is the tallest.)

#### 2. The Synthetic Way (Using Suffixes)
You can also change the ending of the adjective to **-τερος, -τερη, -τερο**.

* **ψηλός** (tall) -> **ψηλότερος** (taller)
* **ωραίος** (beautiful) -> **ωραιότερος** (more beautiful)

> **Note:** For the superlative in this form, we usually just add the definite article: **ο ψηλότερος** (the tallest).

### Negative and Question Forms
* **Negative:** Δεν είναι πιο ψηλός από εμένα. (He is not taller than me.)
* **Question:** Είναι πιο ακριβό από αυτό; (Is it more expensive than this?)

### Exceptions
Some common adjectives have irregular forms:
* **καλός** (good) -> **καλύτερος** (better)
* **κακός** (bad) -> **χειρότερος** (worse)
* **πολύς** (much/many) -> **περισσότερος** (more)
* **μεγάλος** (big/old) -> **μεγαλύτερος** (bigger/older)
