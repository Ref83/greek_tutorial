## The Genitive Case (Η Γενική Πτώση)

### The Concept
The Genitive case is primarily used to show **possession**, **relationship**, or **origin**. It answers the questions "Whose?" (Ποιανού / Ποιανής / Ποιανού) or "Of what?". In Greek, it acts like the English word "of" or the possessive "'s".

---

### Usage
We use the Genitive case in several key scenarios:
* **Possession:** Το σπίτι **του Πέτρου** (Peter's house / The house of Peter).
* **Relationship:** Η μητέρα **της Μαρίας** (Maria's mother).
* **Description/Category:** Ένα ποτήρι **κρασί** (A glass of wine).
* **Time/Quantity:** Ενός **έτους** (Of one year).

---

### The Mechanics


#### 1. Masculine Nouns (ο)
Masculine nouns ending in **-ος** change to **-ου**. Those ending in **-ας** or **-ης** simply drop the **-ς**.

| Ending (Singular) | Genitive Form | Example | Translation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **-ος** | **-ου** | ο φίλος → **του φίλου** | of the friend |
| **-ας** | **-α** | ο πατέρας → **του πατέρα** | of the father |
| **-ης** | **-η** | ο μαθητής → **του μαθητή** | of the student |

**Plural:** All masculine nouns end in **-ων** in the plural Genitive.
* Example: οι δάσκαλοι → **των δασκάλων** (of the teachers).

#### 2. Feminine Nouns (η)
Feminine nouns ending in **-α** or **-η** add a **-ς** at the end.

| Ending (Singular) | Genitive Form | Example | Translation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **-α** | **-ας** | η μητέρα → **της μητέρας** | of the mother |
| **-η** | **-ης** | η τάξη → **της τάξης** | of the class |

**Plural:** All feminine nouns end in **-ων** in the plural Genitive.
* Example: οι γυναίκες → **των γυναικών** (of the women).

#### 3. Neuter Nouns (το)
Neuter nouns ending in **-ο** and **-ι** change to **-ου**. Nouns ending in **-μα** add **-τος**.

| Ending (Singular) | Genitive Form | Example | Translation |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **-ο** | **-ου** | το βιβλίο → **του βιβλίου** | of the book |
| **-ι** | **-ιού** | το παιδί → **του παιδιού** | of the child |
| **-μα** | **-ματος** | το όνομα → **του ονόματος** | of the name |

**Plural:** Most end in **-ων** (or **-των** for -μα nouns).
* Example: τα σπίτια → **των σπιτιών** (of the houses).
* Example: τα ονόματα → **των ονομάτων** (of the names).

---

### Adjectives
Adjectives must agree with the noun they describe in gender, number, and case.
* **Masculine:** του καλού μαθητή (of the good student)
* **Feminine:** της καλής μαθήτριας (of the good student)
* **Neuter:** του καλού παιδιού (of the good child)

---

### Exceptions
* **Proparoxytone Nouns (-ος):** When a masculine or neuter noun has the accent on the third-to-last syllable (e.g., ο **άν**θρωπος), the accent moves down one syllable in the Genitive: **του αν-θρώ-που**.
* **Feminine nouns in -ος:** Even though they are feminine (like η οδός), they follow the masculine -ος pattern: **της οδού**.
