# Grammar Explanation: The Verb "To Be" (Είμαι)

## Overview
This document explains the conjugation and usage of the most fundamental Greek verb, **είμαι**, which is essential for basic communication.

---

## The Concept
In Greek, the verb **είμαι** (to be) is used to express identity, state of being, or location. It is an irregular verb and serves as the foundation for A1 level Greek.

## Usage
The verb **είμαι** is used in the following contexts:
* **Identity and Names:** Identifying yourself or others.
* **Professions:** Stating what someone does for a living.
* **Origin:** Saying where you are from.
* **Location:** Describing where someone or something is situated.
* **Characteristics:** Describing qualities of people or objects.

## The Mechanics
Greek is a "pro-drop" language, meaning the subject pronouns (I, you, he, etc.) are often omitted because the verb ending identifies the subject.

### Positive Form (Present Tense)
| Person | Greek | English |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 1st Singular | **εγώ είμαι** | I am |
| 2nd Singular | **εσύ είσαι** | You are |
| 3rd Singular | **αυτός/αυτή/αυτό είναι** | He/She/It is |
| 1st Plural | **εμείς είμαστε** | We are |
| 2nd Plural | **εσείς είστε / είσαστε** | You are (plural/formal) |
| 3rd Plural | **αυτοί/αυτές/αυτά είναι** | They are |



### Examples
* **Είμαι** ο Γιώργος. (I am George.)
* **Είσαι** στο σπίτι τώρα; (Are you at home now?)
* **Είναι** δάσκαλος. (He is a teacher.)
* **Είμαστε** φίλοι. (We are friends.)

### Negative Form
To form a negative sentence, place the particle **δεν** before the verb.
* Εγώ **δεν είμαι** κουρασμένος. (I am not tired.)

### Question Form
In Greek, there is no change in word order for questions. Use a rising intonation in speech and a Greek question mark (**;**) in writing.
* **Είσαι** καλά; (Are you well?)

## Exceptions
* **Third Person Similarity:** Note that **είναι** is the same for both "he/she/it is" and "they are." Context determines the meaning.
* **Second Person Plural:** Both **είστε** and **είσαστε** are correct and interchangeable, though **είστε** is more common.
