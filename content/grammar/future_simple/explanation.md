## Future Simple (Στιγμιαίος Μέλλοντας)

### The Concept
The **Future Simple** is used to describe an action that will happen **once**, **momentarily**, or at a **specific point** in the future. It is the most common way to express future plans, promises, or intentions in daily conversation.

### Usage
We use this tense when we want to say:
* **A specific plan:** "I will go to the market tomorrow."
* **A single event:** "The concert will start at nine."
* **A promise:** "I will call you later."

### The Mechanics
To form the Future Simple, we follow a simple two-step formula:
1.  Place the particle **θα** (which comes from "θέλω να" – I want to) before the verb.
2.  Use the **Aorist Stem** (the "dependent" or "subjunctive" form) of the verb instead of the present stem.

#### Positive Form
The endings for most regular verbs are the same as the present tense, but the **stem** changes (usually by adding a **-σ-**).

**Example: γράφω (I write) → θα γράψω (I will write)**

| Person | Conjugation | English |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| εγώ | **θα γράψω** | I will write |
| εσύ | **θα γράψεις** | You will write |
| αυτός/ή/ό | **θα γράψει** | He/She/It will write |
| εμείς | **θα γράψουμε** | We will write |
| εσείς | **θα γράψετε** | You (all) will write |
| αυτοί/ές/ά | **θα γράψουν** | They will write |


#### Negative Form
To say you will *not* do something, simply place **δεν** before the particle **θα**.
* **Δεν θα φάω** το μήλο. (I will not eat the apple.)

#### Question Form
Just like in the present tense, we simply change our intonation or add a question mark at the end.
* **Θα πας** στο σινεμά; (Will you go to the cinema?)

### The Stem Change
For regular verbs, the most common change to the stem is:
* **-π-, -β-, -φ-, -πτω** → change to **-ψ-** (e.g., *γράφω* → *θα γράψω*)
* **-κ-, -γ-, -χ-, -χνω** → change to **-ξ-** (e.g., *ανοίγω* → *θα ανοίξω*)
* **-τ-, -δ-, -θ-, -ζω** → change to **-σ-** (e.g., *διαβάζω* → *θα διαβάσω*)

### Exceptions (Irregular Verbs)
Some of the most common verbs have unique stems that you must memorize:

* **είμαι** (to be) → **θα είμαι** (I will be)
* **έχω** (to have) → **θα έχω** (I will have)
* **πάω/πηγαίνω** (to go) → **θα πάω** (I will go)
* **τρώω** (to eat) → **θα φάω** (I will eat)
* **πίνω** (to drink) → **θα πιω** (I will drink)
* **βλέπω** (to see) → **θα δω** (I will see)
* **λέω** (to say) → **θα πω** (I will say)

**Examples of Intentions:**
1. **Θα ταξιδέψω** στην Ελλάδα το καλοκαίρι. (I will travel to Greece in the summer.)
2. Αύριο **θα αγοράσω** ένα καινούριο βιβλίο. (Tomorrow I will buy a new book.)
3. **Θα φάμε** σε ένα εστιατόριο απόψε. (We will eat at a restaurant tonight.)
